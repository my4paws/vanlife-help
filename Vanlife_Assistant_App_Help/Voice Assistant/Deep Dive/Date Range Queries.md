# 📅 Date Range Queries

A lot of useful voice questions depend on time.

You might ask about:
- spending this month
- fuel costs last 30 days
- trips taken this year
- food spending between June and August

The assistant is designed to understand many of these natural date phrases automatically.

If you are new to voice features, start with **[[Voice_Overview|Voice Overview]]**.

---

## Common examples

The assistant can usually understand phrases like:
- this week
- this month
- last month
- this year
- last year
- past 30 days
- last 6 months
- between January and March
- in 2024

---

## Rolling ranges

A rolling range moves with time.

For example:
- last 30 days
- past 90 days
- last 6 months

These are especially useful when you want a moving picture of spending and travel patterns rather than a fixed calendar month.

---

## Why date ranges matter

They let you ask natural questions instead of building filters by hand.

Examples:
- “How much did I spend on fuel last month?”
- “Show campsite spending this year.”
- “How much did I spend in Spain over the last 90 days?”

If you want to refine the result afterwards, see **[[Follow Up Questions]]**.

For the deeper parser behaviour, read **[[Understanding Date Ranges]]**.
