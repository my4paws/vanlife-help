# 📷 My scanner isn't recognising the QR code

Sometimes the scanner just refuses to play ball. That is frustrating when you are standing in the van with a box in one hand and your phone in the other, but in most cases the cause is simple.

If you are new to this part of the app, start with **[[Understanding_the_Manifest|Understanding the Manifest]]** and **[[Containers_and_QR_Labels|Containers and QR Labels]]** so the overall system makes sense.

---

## Quick checks

### Lighting

QR labels scan best in even light. Harsh glare, deep shadow, or a shiny laminated surface can all make the code harder for the camera to read.

### Distance

If the label fills the whole screen, the camera may struggle to focus properly.

Try moving the phone slightly further back, then hold still for a second so autofocus can settle.

### Label quality

A label that is creased, scratched, dusty, smudged, or printed too small may not scan reliably.

If needed, print a fresh label and compare it with the old one. The full setup flow is covered in **[[Containers_and_QR_Labels|Containers and QR Labels]]**.

### Curved or awkward surfaces

Labels wrapped around a rounded tub, soft bag, or uneven cupboard edge are often harder to scan than labels stuck to a flat face.

---

## Still not working?

You can still manage things manually.

Try:
- opening the container directly in Manifest
- searching for the item by name
- checking whether the container is in the correct zone
- reprinting the label at a slightly larger size

If you are still building out your storage layout, **[[Setting_Up_Your_Vans_Zones|Setting Up Your Van's Zones]]** is also worth a look.

---

## A practical vanlife tip

Matte labels usually behave better than glossy ones, especially in bright sunshine or under spot lighting inside the van.
