# 🏷️ Vendors vs. Categories: The "Who" and the "What"

When you’re tracking finances on the road, it’s easy for your ledger to become a chaotic mess of grocery receipts and fuel stops. To keep your data clean and your budget accurate, Vanlife Assistant uses a two-tier system: **Vendors** and **Categories**.

Understanding the difference is the key to knowing whether you can afford that next border crossing!

---

## 🏬 Vendors: The "Who" (or "Where")
A **Vendor** is the specific shop, person, or service you gave your money to. 

* **Examples:** *Shell, Tesco, Decathlon, Blue Bear Cafe, or even "Dave the Mechanic."*
* **How it works:** When you log a transaction, the Vendor field is a free-text box. You can type whatever you want! 
* **The Magic of Suggestions:** You don't have to type the same name every time. The app looks back at your last 500 entries and offers **Historical Suggestions** as you type. Tap a suggestion to instantly fill the Vendor, Category, and Account fields based on your past habits.

> 💡 **Pro-Tip: Be Consistent!**
> Try to use the same name for the same shop (e.g., use "Shell" instead of "Shell Station 104"). This makes it incredibly easy to use the Search Bar later to see exactly how much you've spent at that specific merchant.

---

## 📂 Categories: The "What" (and "Why")
A **Category** defines the *purpose* of the expense. This is what powers your budget bars and your 10-year runway forecast.

* **Examples:** *Fuel, Groceries, Van Maintenance, or Subscriptions.*
* **Category Groups:** To keep things organized, categories are bundled into **Groups** (like "Transport" or "Living"). This allows the app to show you a "Sparkline Trend" of your overall transport costs without you getting bogged down in individual receipts.
* **Icons & Colors:** Every category has a unique icon and every group has a color. This gives your ledger a "Visual Grammar"—you'll soon be able to spot an expensive fuel month just by seeing the amount of blue in your charts!.

> ✨ **Magic Feature: Vehicle Tracking**
> Some categories are "Vehicle Aware" (look for the little blue truck icon! 🚛). When you pick a category like **Fuel** or **Service**, the app automatically asks for your **Odometer Reading**. This is how we calculate your fuel efficiency and maintenance alerts!.

---

## ⚖️ Why use both?

Think of it this way:
* **The Category** tells the app's engine how to calculate your **Runway**. 
* **The Vendor** tells *you* exactly where that money went.

If you buy engine oil at a supermarket, the **Vendor** is "Tesco," but the **Category** should be "Van Maintenance." By separating these, you can still see that you are over budget on "Maintenance" while knowing exactly which shop you went to for the parts.
